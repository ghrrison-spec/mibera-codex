# Cross

## Visual Elements

![Cross](https://mibera.fsn1.your-objectstorage.com/components/face%20accessories__z60/SS3_milady_Cross.webp)

Slightly transparent upside down cross.

Dominant colors: Black

## Cultural Context

The inverted cross (Cross of Saint Peter) has a dual history: in Catholic tradition, it represents humility, as the apostle Peter requested to be crucified upside down, feeling unworthy of dying in the same manner as Christ. In modern counterculture, heavy metal, and post-ironic internet aesthetics, the inverted cross has been adopted as an anti-establishment or edgy symbol, often stripped of its original religious meaning.

## Justification

An inverted cross on the face sits at the intersection of Catholic humility and counterculture provocation -- the kind of symbol that means different things to different people, which is exactly what makes it right for the Milady archetype.
