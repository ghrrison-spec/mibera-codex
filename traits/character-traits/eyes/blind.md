## Cultural Context

Blind eyes present as completely white, with no visible iris or pupil. Visual impairment has been represented in mythology and literature as both a loss and a gift — from Homer's blind bard tradition to the archetype of the seer who gains inner vision by losing outer sight. The inclusion of disability representation in generative art collections reflects growing awareness of diverse human experience.
