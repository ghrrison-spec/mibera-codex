## Cultural Context

Mahogany refers to the tropical hardwood native to the Americas, prized for its deep reddish-brown color. The tree held sacred status in Mayan culture, connecting this tone to Mesoamerican ancestral lineages. As a skin descriptor, mahogany denotes a warm, deep brown complexion with distinctive reddish or coppery undertones.
