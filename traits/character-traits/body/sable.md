## Cultural Context

Sable takes its name from the animal whose luxurious dark fur was among the most traded goods across Eurasian trade routes, connecting Siberian indigenous peoples with European and Asian markets. As a skin tone, sable describes a rich, dark brown complexion with warm undertones, occupying the warmer end of the deeper tonal range.
