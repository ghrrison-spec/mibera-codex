## Cultural Context

Sami Reindeer Herders of Lapland
Exploring the traditional lifestyle of the Sámi, an Indigenous people of northern Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Russia, focusing on their ancient practice of reindeer herding. It details their seasonal migration, using the term "bozaovazzi" (reindeer walkers), and the cultural significance of reindeer, which provide food, clothing, and shelter. The piece highlights challenges from climate change, like hardened snow affecting reindeer foraging, and industrial impacts like logging and mining. It also promotes cultural immersion through visits to places like Inari, offering experiences like dog-sledding and meeting herders.
