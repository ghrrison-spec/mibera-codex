# Midas in Pactolus

## Visual Elements

![Midas in Pactolus](https://mibera.fsn1.your-objectstorage.com/components/background__z10/midas%20in%20pactolus.webp)

Painterly scenery of a pond / lake with greenish water, surrounded by rocks and greeneries. An old man wearing all earthy-brown clothing can be seen scooping the water.

Dominant colors: Black, muted brown, bright light yellow.

## Cultural Context

King Midas, granted his wish to turn everything he touched to gold, found it a curse as he could neither eat nor drink. Bacchus instructed him to wash in the Pactolus River to reverse it. In Poussin’s painting, a humbled Midas stands behind a river god and putti, symbolizing the river’s source and Bacchus. Created after Poussin’s arrival in Rome, it was among the first artworks acquired by The Met in 1871.

A parable about the curse of turning everything to gold — resonant for a crypto-native project. Based on the Poussin painting, one of the first artworks acquired by The Met in 1871.
