## Cultural Context

Gaia is the Greek primordial goddess of the Earth — mother of the Titans, consort of Uranus (Sky). In Greek cosmogony, Gaia and Uranus are the original pairing from which everything descends. The Gaia and Uranus grails are designed as a combined piece — when Uranus is placed on top of Gaia, they form a single unified artwork, mirroring the cosmological relationship of sky over earth.

## Visual Elements

The earth.

## Justification

Gaia and Uranus represent the primordial pairing — earth and sky — that precedes the planetary and zodiac systems in Greek cosmogony.
