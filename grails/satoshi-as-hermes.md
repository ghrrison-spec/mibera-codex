## Cultural Context

Hermes is the Greek god of messengers, boundaries, commerce, and thieves — and the archetypal trickster mediator of information systems. In "TechGnosis: Myth, Magic, and Mysticism in the Age of Information" (1998), Erik Davis argues that cybernetic systems embody a Hermetic logic — that Hermes is effectively the patron figure of networked communication. Davis's concept of the "hermetic imagination" describes how the ancient role of Hermes as boundary-crosser and messenger maps directly onto digital information networks. Drawing the line from Hermes to Satoshi Nakamoto — the pseudonymous, unidentified creator of Bitcoin — extends that logic: the messenger god who mediates between worlds becomes the anonymous architect who mediates trustless exchange between strangers.

A statue of Satoshi Nakamoto was erected in Lugano, Switzerland, a city that has adopted Bitcoin as legal tender. The statue is constructed from horizontal metal panels stacked with gaps between them. From the side, the figure appears solid. From the front, only the thin edges of each panel are visible — the figure reduces to a series of horizontal lines.

## Visual Elements

Satoshi depicted as Hermes, inspired by the front view of the Lugano statue — the angle at which the figure disappears. One hidden message in the piece.

## Justification

Satoshi is a cypherpunk. Cypherpunk representation is central to Mibera.
