## Cultural Context

The radiate crown originates from Roman imperial iconography, most associated with Sol Invictus (the "Unconquered Sun"). Radiate crowns with pointed rays signified divinity and, in later Roman and early Christian contexts, resurrection. 126.22 Hz is the Sun's frequency in Hans Cousto's Cosmic Octave system, which derives audible tones from planetary orbital periods.

## Visual Elements

8-pronged radiate crown signifying resurrection. 126.22 Hz solar frequency displayed in the background and repeated to illustrate how the sun's energy travels. Colors meant to be reminiscent of how it looks to look at the sun through closed eyes.

## Justification

The planet and luminary grails represent the celestial bodies as their own identities — the sources of the energy that the zodiac system channels.
