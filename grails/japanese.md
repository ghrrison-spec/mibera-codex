## Cultural Context

Ukiyo-e ("pictures of the floating world") is a genre of Japanese woodblock prints produced from the 17th through 19th centuries. Inro are decorative Japanese containers traditionally carried at the waist to hold medicines and personal seals. Torii gates mark the transition from the mundane to the sacred at Shinto shrines. Senjafuda are name stickers that visitors traditionally post at temples and shrines.
