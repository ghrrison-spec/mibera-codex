## Cultural Context

Odin is the Norse god of wisdom, knowledge, and death — the Norse equivalent of Mercury/Hermes. Both serve as psychopomps (guides of souls between worlds) and are associated with language and knowledge. The day Wednesday (Old English Wōdnesdæg, "Odin's day") corresponds to Mercury's day in Romance languages (French Mercredi). Huginn and Muninn ("Thought" and "Memory") are Odin's two ravens who fly across the world each day and report back to him. Gungnir is Odin's spear. The caduceus is the staff of Hermes/Mercury.
