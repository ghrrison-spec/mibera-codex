## Cultural Context

Mongolian petroglyphs (cave art) date back thousands of years, depicting animals, hunters, and ritual scenes across Central Asia and Siberia. The morin khuur (horsehead fiddle) is a traditional Mongolian bowed string instrument, considered a symbol of the Mongolian nation. Khoomei (throat singing) is a vocal technique where the singer produces multiple simultaneous tones — recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage.

## Visual Elements

Simplified Mongolian cave art style. Holding a morin khuur. Circles moving outward from the figure to represent sound and throat singing.
